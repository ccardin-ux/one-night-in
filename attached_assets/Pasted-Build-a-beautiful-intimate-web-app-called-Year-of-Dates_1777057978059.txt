Build a beautiful, intimate web app called Year of Dates for Seth and Elana, a newly married couple, to help them create and enjoy one meaningful date night every month during their first year of marriage.

This app should feel like a wedding gift, a relationship ritual, and a practical planning tool all at once. It should help them explore the world together through food, music, conversation, and playful shared experiences — without making date night feel like work.

Who it is for

Seth loves to cook, so food should be a central part of the experience.
Elana is a DJ, so music should feel expressive, curated, and personal.
They are both deep thinkers, big communicators, and not afraid of emotionally rich or wide-ranging conversations. The app should embrace that. It should not oversimplify them or make the experience feel shallow.

Core concept

Each month, the app should generate or present one complete date night plan built around a destination, culture, or theme. Every monthly date should feel like a small adventure they can actually pull off in real life.

Each date night should include:

a monthly theme or destination
a dish or meal idea Seth can cook
a music direction or playlist inspiration for Elana
a simple ritual to begin the evening
a set of thoughtful conversation questions
a fun, surprising, or unique shared activity
an easy way to schedule it on the calendar
a light prep checklist
a place to capture memories, notes, and what they learned about each other
What success looks like

The app should make it easy for Seth and Elana to:

keep date night consistent for 12 months
spend less time planning and more time enjoying
have deeper, more memorable conversations
explore new places, ideas, and cultures together
create a meaningful first-year keepsake of their marriage
Product experience

The app should center around a 12-month journey.

They should be able to:

See all 12 months at a glance
Open the current month’s date night
View a complete plan for the evening
Add it to their calendar
Check off any prep steps
Use the app during the date if needed
Save reflections afterward
Monthly date structure

Each month should feel fully curated and easy to act on. Every monthly date page should include:

Month name
Theme / destination / concept
Short intro explaining the vibe of the month
Dinner idea with a cuisine or dish Seth can make
Music inspiration Elana can turn into a playlist
Opening ritual to mark the beginning of the night
Conversation prompts designed for people who like to go deep and wide
One fun activity that feels fresh, playful, or unexpected
Optional local add-on if they want to leave the house
Prep checklist
Estimated effort, cost, and time
Add to calendar action
Post-date reflection section
Tone and design direction

The app should feel:

warm
elegant
romantic
thoughtful
premium but not flashy
emotionally intelligent
easy to use

It should feel more like a carefully designed keepsake than a generic productivity tool.

Important product principles
Keep the scope focused on 12 monthly date nights
Prioritize clarity and execution over too many options
Make each month feel curated, not random
Keep planning friction low
Make every date feel special but still realistic
Support deep conversation without making the app feel like therapy
Let food, music, ritual, and play work together as one experience
Functional requirements

Include:

a homepage or dashboard with all 12 monthly dates
one featured date night plan per month
the ability to swap or regenerate a monthly theme
a clear Add to Calendar action
a prep checklist
a reflection / memory capture flow
a section that tracks what we’re learning about each other
saved favorite rituals, activities, or prompts
Explicitly out of scope

Do not make this:

a generic couples app
a therapy or counseling product
a travel booking platform
a social network
an endless feed of recommendations

Keep it focused on a single, beautiful promise:

Help Seth and Elana enjoy one easy, meaningful, world-inspired date night every month during their first year of marriage.
